CLIMATE CHANGE

Climate change is the long-term alteration of Earth's temperature, weather patterns, and environmental conditions. While natural factors have influenced the climate throughout history, scientific evidence shows that human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes are now the primary drivers of rapid climate change. These activities increase the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, causing global temperatures to rise.
